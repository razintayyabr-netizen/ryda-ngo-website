About RYDA

A Rohingya-led civil society organization centered on rights, representation, and protection.

The Rohingya Youth Development Association works exclusively for the Rohingya community. RYDA conducts comprehensive human rights advocacy, documents and publicly reports violations, carries out in-depth research, and advances community-led engagement for accountability and justice.

The organization actively promotes freedom of expression, association, and movement even in highly restrictive environments, while creating space for youth, girls, and women to lead initiatives and participate in decision-making.

What RYDA Does

Through documentation, reporting, advocacy, research, leadership development, and community engagement, RYDA strengthens resilience, representation, empowerment, and protection of Rohingya rights and dignity at local, national, and international levels.

RYDA also responds to emergencies with humanitarian support including food, shelter, medical care, psychosocial assistance, safe drinking water, sanitation, and essential supplies for vulnerable populations in refugee camps and crisis-affected areas.

Vision

RYDA seeks a peaceful, just, and sustainable future where Rohingya communities live with dignity, equality, security, and full respect for their human rights.

Why This Matters

Rohingya-led evidence, advocacy, and protection are essential to accountability.

By combining human rights documentation, international engagement, leadership development, research, community participation, and humanitarian response, RYDA helps Rohingya youth, girls, and women become responsible leaders shaping a more just future.

Strategic Action

An integrated model that links evidence, advocacy, and emergency response.

RYDA does not treat rights work and humanitarian response as separate tracks. The organization connects field realities, community voice, and global engagement so protection efforts are informed by lived experience and verified evidence.

Systematic Documentation

Violations are documented, verified, and preserved to support reporting, accountability efforts, and informed advocacy.

Public & International Advocacy

Reports, briefings, campaigns, and engagement with international mechanisms help carry Rohingya demands for justice onto global platforms.

Protection & Care

Emergency assistance prioritizes women, children, older people, and persons with disabilities through care, shelter, nutrition, water, hygiene, and psychosocial support.

Rights To Action

A strategic pathway from evidence gathering to protection, justice, and leadership.

RYDA's work follows a practical chain: document realities, report them credibly, elevate community voices, and translate advocacy into protection and long-term resilience.

01

02

03

04

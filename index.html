About RYDA

A Rohingya-led civil society organization built on rights, representation, and protection.

RYDA conducts comprehensive human rights advocacy, documents and publicly reports violations, carries out in-depth research, and advances community-led engagement for accountability and justice.

The organization actively promotes freedom of expression, association, and movement — even in highly restrictive environments — while creating space for youth, girls, and women to lead.

Vision
"A peaceful, just, and sustainable future where Rohingya communities live with dignity, equality, security, and full respect for their human rights."
Programs & Activities

Eight integrated programs linking evidence, advocacy, and emergency response.

01

Human Rights Documentation & Reporting

Systematic documentation, verification, and preservation of evidence on killings, arbitrary detention, gender-based violence, and restrictions on fundamental freedoms.

02

Advocacy & International Engagement

Sustained advocacy with global human rights bodies, public campaigns, and outreach to advance accountability, protection, and durable solutions.

03

Youth Leadership & Capacity Building

Training, mentorship, and leadership development for Rohingya youths, girls, and women to lead initiatives and participate in advocacy and community action.

04

Research & Analysis

In-depth research on human rights conditions and evidence-based analysis that support advocacy, policy recommendations, and informed decision-making.

05

Community Engagement & Participation

Consultations, participatory research, and community-led initiatives that ensure Rohingya priorities and lived experiences shape the response.

06

Campaigns & Awareness Raising

Advocacy campaigns, media engagement, and awareness initiatives that amplify Rohingya voices and draw attention to violations and systemic discrimination.

07

Accountability & Justice Initiatives

Documentation and reporting processes that contribute to legal action, accountability efforts, and international justice mechanisms.

08

Humanitarian Assistance in Emergencies

Food, safe water, temporary shelter, hygiene support, medical care, psychosocial assistance, and essential supplies for the most vulnerable.

Core Values

The principles that guide RYDA's advocacy, documentation, and humanitarian action.

Human Dignity

Upholding the inherent dignity and fundamental rights of Rohingya communities in every action.

Youth Empowerment

Rohingya youths, girls, and women supported as leaders, organizers, and agents of change.

Integrity & Accountability

Transparency, ethical responsibility, and evidence-based work shape RYDA's reporting and advocacy.

Community Participation

Community voices, experiences, and priorities guide RYDA through consultation and participatory action.

Equality & Justice

Standing against discrimination, working for fairness, equal access to rights, and justice.

Humanitarian Assistance

Urgent support delivered with care and dignity, prioritizing those facing the greatest risk.
